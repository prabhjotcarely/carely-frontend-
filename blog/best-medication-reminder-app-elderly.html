2. Medisafe
⭐ 4.7 · 200,000+ reviews · 10M downloads
Free + $4.99/mo

Medisafe is the market leader for a reason — it's reliable, well-designed, and has a huge feature set including drug interaction checking and pharmacy refill integration. But it's built for individual users managing their own medications, not families managing an elderly relative's care from a distance.

PROS

  • Most reliable reminders
  • Drug interaction checker
  • Pharmacy integration
  • 10M users — proven and stable

CONS

  • No AI features
  • Cluttered UI — hard for elderly users
  • Family coordination is clunky
  • No real-time family alerts
Best for: Adults managing their own medications who want the most feature-complete reminder app available.
3. CareZone
⭐ 4.7 historically · Now abandoned
FreeABANDONED ⚠️

CareZone was once a strong option for family caregivers. Amazon acquired the technology in 2020 and the app has had no meaningful updates since. Users report persistent bugs, missing features, and zero support. We don't recommend relying on it for anything important.

PROS

  • Free
  • Once had good family coordination

CONS

  • Effectively abandoned by Amazon
  • Bugs unfixed for years
  • Zero customer support
  • No new features since 2021
Verdict: Do not rely on CareZone for managing a loved one's medications. Look for a CareZone alternative.
4. MyTherapy
⭐ 4.7 · 100,000+ reviews
Free

MyTherapy is a clean, well-designed individual medication tracker. It's free and works well for personal use. But it's made by a German company with no North America-specific features and no family coordination capability.

PROS

  • Free
  • Clean UI
  • Symptom and mood journal
  • Doctor-shareable health reports

CONS

  • No caregiver or family features
  • No AI
  • No Canada/US pharmacy integration
  • Individual use only
Best for: Individuals managing their own health who want a free journaling + reminder app.
5. Hero Health
⭐ 4.5 · Smart pill dispenser
$49.99/mo + hardware

Hero Health is a hardware-first solution — an automated pill dispenser that sorts medications and alerts family when a dose is missed. Impressive technology, but the cost ($600/year minimum, plus the hardware device) makes it out of reach for most families.

PROS

  • Automated physical dispensing
  • Strong family alert system
  • Handles up to 10 medications

CONS

  • $600+/year cost
  • Requires hardware purchase & setup
  • Not portable
  • No AI conversation features
Best for: Families who have budget and want the most hands-off physical solution for a loved one who lives alone.
