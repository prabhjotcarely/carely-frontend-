The specific problem Alzheimer's and dementia create

Other medication reminder problems are about forgetting. The Alzheimer's problem is different — it's about not knowing whether you forgot. Your parent may genuinely believe they took their medication when they didn't. Or take it twice because they don't remember the first time.

Carely solves this by creating a real-time confirmation loop. Elara asks. Your parent responds. That response is the log. You see it immediately. The family sees it. The ambiguity is gone.

Common Questions

My parent with dementia is not good with technology. Can they still use Carely?
Carely is designed to be as simple as possible for the patient — large buttons, a single tap to confirm, and Elara's warm messages that feel conversational rather than technical. Most families set up the app on the parent's phone and manage everything remotely. The parent just needs to respond to Elara's messages.
What if my parent takes a medication twice by mistake?
Carely logs every confirmation. If your parent responds "taken" twice to the same reminder, both responses are timestamped. You can see the log immediately. Carely does not prevent double-dosing, but it gives you and the care team visibility to catch it.
Can we connect my parent's neurologist and GP?
Yes. Carely can send weekly adherence reports to connected doctors. Add as many healthcare providers as needed. Each receives the same weekly report with medication adherence data and vitals readings.
My parent lives in a memory care facility. Is Carely still useful?
Carely is most valuable for parents living at home or semi-independently. In a full memory care facility, medications are typically administered by staff. Carely may still be useful for tracking vitals and staying connected, but the core medication reminder feature is designed for home settings.

Yes. Built for both USA and Canada. The founding team is based in BC, Canada.
