Medicine tracker vs. medication reminder — what's the difference?

A medication reminder tells you when to take something. A medicine tracker records whether you actually did, keeps a history, and makes that data useful.

Carely does both. Elara reminds you. The log records the result. The report shares the data with your doctor. The alert notifies your family. It's a complete system — not just a notification.

Common Questions

Can I track both prescription medications and supplements in one log?
Yes. Carely makes no distinction between prescription medications and supplements. They all live in the same schedule, the same dose log, and the same weekly doctor report. One app for everything you take.
How far back does the medicine log go?
Carely stores your full dose history from the day you start. There is no rolling 30-day limit. You can reference any dose taken, skipped, or missed since you created your account.
Can my family see my medicine log?
Yes — only the family members or caregivers you explicitly connect. Each connected person sees the same real-time log. They also receive push notifications if a dose is missed.
Will my doctor see the medicine log?
Yes. Carely automatically sends a weekly adherence report to the connected doctor. The report includes every medicine tracked, dose taken vs. missed, and vitals readings — formatted and ready to review.

Yes. Carely is built for both the USA and Canada. The founding team is based in BC, Canada. Pricing available in USD and CAD.
