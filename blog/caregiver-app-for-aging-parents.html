Common Questions

My parent is resistant to technology. Will they actually use Carely?
Carely is designed to be as simple as possible for the parent. Elara's message feels like a text, not a medical app. The parent taps one button — that's it. You handle all the setup. Most families find that once the parent sees Elara's warm morning message, they respond naturally. The simple experience wins over parents who resist "apps."
My parent lives with their spouse. Can the spouse also use Carely?
Yes. The spouse can be set up on the same family network as a connected caregiver — so they also receive alerts and can see the dashboard. If the parent isn't responding to Elara, the spouse can check in immediately. Both are part of the same care network.
What if my parent sometimes refuses to take their medications?
Carely gives your parent the option to respond "Skip" — which is logged and flagged in the family dashboard. You can see when they chose not to take a medication, not just when they forgot. This is information you can bring to the doctor or discuss with your parent directly.
How is this different from just calling my parent every day?
A phone call tells you they're alive. Carely tells you they took their Warfarin at 8:47am. It's a fundamentally different level of information. You still call — but you call knowing whether you need to worry, not hoping they remember to tell you they took their pills.
Is Carely available in Canada?
Yes. Built for both USA and Canada. The founding team is based in BC, Canada.
