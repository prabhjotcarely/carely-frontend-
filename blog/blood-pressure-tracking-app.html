Why logging alone isn't enough

Most blood pressure apps let your parent log readings. That's it. The reading sits in a database no one looks at until the next doctor's visit — three months later.

By then, if your parent's blood pressure has been running at 160/100 for six weeks, the damage is already done. Carely is the first blood pressure tracking app that alerts the people who need to know — in real time.

How it works

1
Elara prompts your parent at the right time
You set a schedule — morning, after medication, before bed. Elara sends a warm reminder to log their reading at that time every day.
2

They check their blood pressure monitor (any brand, no Bluetooth required) and type the systolic and diastolic numbers into Carely. Takes 15 seconds.

Common Questions

What blood pressure monitors work with Carely?
All of them. Carely uses manual entry — your parent reads the number from any monitor and types it in. No Bluetooth, no brand compatibility issues. Any upper-arm or wrist monitor works.
Can I see a history of my parent's blood pressure readings?
Yes. The caregiver dashboard shows the full reading history with timestamps. You can see trends over days and weeks — not just the most recent number.
Does Carely recommend medications or treatment based on BP readings?
Never. Carely flags readings and alerts family. Any high-reading notification includes a recommendation to consult a doctor. Carely is a monitoring tool, not a diagnostic tool.
Can I track blood pressure for two parents in the same app?
Yes. Carely supports multiple patient profiles. If you're managing medications and vitals for both parents, add them both. Each has a separate schedule, log, and alert settings.
Is Carely available in Canada?
Yes. Carely is built for both the USA and Canada. The founding team is based in BC, Canada.
